# SEO & Discoverability

Ambient channels grow by winning YouTube **search first**, then converting it into **suggested/autoplay** session chains. A brand-new channel has zero algorithmic momentum, so the only cold-start lever is ranking for high-intent long-tail keywords. Sequence accordingly.

## Keyword research (budget toolkit)

YouTube Search autocomplete (most reliable free source — snowball: seed → harvest → re-seed); Google Trends (YouTube-search filter for seasonality + head-to-head); vidIQ free (keyword score + competitor tag overlay — most generous); TubeBuddy free; keywordtool.io; competitor title/tag mining. **Caveat:** YouTube publishes no volumes; every number is an estimate and tools disagree (YouTube vs Google volume can differ ~50x). Enter on lower-competition long-tail where top results have <~50k–100k views.

- **Rainy Cabin:** head (HIGH competition, don't lead) — "rain sounds," "rain sounds for sleeping," "rain and thunder," "thunderstorm sounds." Modifiers that move volume: duration ("10 hours"/"3 hours"/"8 hours"), use-case ("for sleeping/studying/relaxation"), format ("black screen"), texture ("rain on window/tin roof"). Long-tail entry: "rain on cabin window at night for sleeping," "cozy cabin rain and fireplace 10 hours black screen," "rain on cabin roof no thunder." Dead-ends: bare "rain sounds"/"white noise."
- **Sleep/Ambient Music:** high-volume/high-difficulty — "sleep music," "deep sleep music," "relaxing music," "calming music" (heavy incumbents — Yellow Brick Cinema, Jason Stephenson). Modifiers: "deep sleep," "for sleeping/anxiety/studying," duration stacks, frequency terms ("528hz/432hz/delta waves/binaural beats" — long-tail content categories, unproven as volume drivers; use as tags, **avoid health claims**). Long-tail entry: "guided sleep meditation," "guided meditation for anxiety," "528hz sleep music for anxiety."
- **Pet (Dog):** "music for dogs," "calming music for dogs," "music for dogs to sleep," "separation anxiety music for dogs." Modifiers: "separation anxiety," "to sleep," "for puppies," "while you're away," "fireworks," "thunderstorm anxiety," duration (12–20h). Long-tail entry: "calming music for dogs with separation anxiety while at work," "music to calm dogs during fireworks." Acute, specific intent a new channel can win.

## Title formulas

Structure: **front-loaded primary keyword → benefit/use-case → texture/descriptor → duration → format modifier**, <~100 chars (60–70 visible safest). Pipe `|` convention; brackets for duration/format. Front-load the primary keyword; include duration; don't keyword-stuff (reads as spam, underperforms). **Titles must be accurate** (misleading-metadata policy is enforced — say thunder → there's thunder; "10 hours" → it's 10 hours; "no ads" → no mid-rolls).
- *Rainy Cabin:* "Rain Sounds for Sleeping 🌧️ Heavy Rain & Thunder on Cabin Window | 10 Hours Black Screen"
- *Sleep Music:* "Deep Sleep Music 528Hz | Calming Delta Waves for Anxiety & Insomnia | 8 Hours Black Screen"
- *Dog:* "Calming Music for Dogs with Separation Anxiety 🐶 Music to Relax Dogs Home Alone | 12 Hours"
- **Shorts differ:** separate algorithm (completion/loop, not search) — short/hooky/benefit-led, 1–2 hashtags. The Short's job is reach + funnel, not keyword ranking.

## Metadata for speechless content

With no speech, auto-captions produce nothing — **title + description + chapters + thumbnail carry 100% of the semantic load** (description matters MORE for ambient).
- First 1–2 lines (~150 chars before "Show more"): primary keyword in a natural sentence + core benefit (appears in the search snippet).
- Body (200–500 words): describe soundscape/scene/use-cases; weave semantic variations ("rainstorm," "downpour," "thunderstorm," "white noise," "pink noise," "ambient sleep sounds") — the transcript substitute.
- Tags: YouTube says "minimal role" — ~30 seconds, 5–8 focused tags, first = exact target keyword. Don't stuff 30.
- Hashtags: 3–5 max at description end (first 3 render above the title); genre/intent.
- Per-video variation: descriptions materially varied (boilerplate intro/outro fine, substance differs) — the review gate enforces (copy-paste metadata trips the inauthentic-content rule).

## Playlists, end screens, chapters, session engineering (core of the model)

- **Playlists:** intent-based ("Rain for Deep Sleep," "Study & Focus Rain," "8-Hour Overnight Loops"; dogs: "Separation Anxiety Relief," "Fireworks Calm"). Playlists rank in search (a second surface — keyword the title). One video lives in multiple. They increase session watch time (heavily rewarded) and show as their own traffic source. Don't make 100-video lists.
- **End screens (last 5–20s; ≥25s video):** best layout is NOT the 4-grid — **one video element ("Best for viewer") + one subscribe button** (CTR ~6–10%, ~2x the grid). End-screen clicks = session continuation (strong positive signal). Requires the "outro plate" (visual quiet in the right two-thirds for the last ~20s) built into the loop — see `references/pipeline.md`.
- **Chapters (use with care):** need 00:00 first + ≥3 + each ≥10s. Help on **study/focus and videos that vary across runtime** (indexable keyword text + navigation); **minimize on pure "fall asleep" loops** (they invite skipping that fragments the long watch). Descriptive keyword titles, never "Part 1/Part 2."

## Search vs suggested vs browse — sequencing

- **Cold-start:** no co-visitation data → the algorithm defaults to search-query matching + small test batches; Suggested is hard to trigger early. **Search is the only reliable lever.**
- **At scale:** watch-time + topical authority build your "neighbourhood" and Suggested/Browse take over ("Suggested is the #1 traffic source by total views" for mature channels).
- **Thesis:** Phase 1 (launch → first few thousand watch-hours) — optimize HARD for search (low-competition long-tail, front-loaded titles, keyword-rich first-line descriptions, keyword-titled playlists). Phase 2 (search + retention proven) — pivot to session-engineering for suggested (end screens, playlist chaining, tight niche focus, thumbnail/CTR). Diagnostics (Studio → Reach → Traffic source types, weekly): Search >50% with weak Suggested = content isn't triggering the chain (fix retention/CTR/neighbourhood); External >50% = low CTR.

## Highest-ROI moves & mistakes

- **Moves:** front-loaded accurate keyword-rich titles; first-line keyword-rich descriptions; keyword-titled intent playlists; "Best for viewer" + subscribe end-screen on every video; target "black screen"/duration/use-case modifiers; per-video variation (compliant + its own keyword target).
- **Mistakes:** leading with saturated head terms; keyword-stuffing; copy-paste metadata (trips inauthentic-content, applies channel-wide); misleading titles/durations (strikes); treating tags as important; no playlists/end screens (leaving the session chain on the table); inconsistent niche (prevents the suggested neighbourhood); ignoring the Reach tab.

## Caveats

- No exact tool-attributed YouTube volumes exist; validate in-tool. Frequency/wave claims (528Hz/delta) aren't robustly evidence-backed — categories, not health claims. Traffic-mix benchmarks are aggregated estimates. The inauthentic-content rule is the dominant risk — genuine per-video variation is non-negotiable.
